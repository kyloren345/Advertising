Pros

Nowadays we have zero emmision buses that are starting to pop up around Melbourne and around the world and they solve part 1 of the problem, however, this still leaves the issue of having to stress about paying everytime you take public transport. Although the only method of companies making a profit over their deficits is to keep this payment, it could help many people travel with less stress and also just not have to spend so much on transport alone. Think about this, someone who lives in the Central Bureau District probably wouldn't invest in getting a car as their destinations are at either walking distance or would just require them to take a taxi. Whatever the case, if they chose public transport every time, they could end up spending over the $2403.27 that someoe who lived in a region further from their destinations. After just a year of taking public transport, a person in high-school could have payed off their tuition for a single subject if they hadn't and after 10 or so years, they could have payed off their entire tuition fees for that year. Especially if someone wanted to buy their first car they could have bought one after nine years of savings from not using paid transport.

Cons

There are only a few major cons to having free public transport and one of those is that; the government and companies that run public transport won't receive any income from people that use their services. This could be a slight problem and could be solved by making paid public transport optional or even just having more sponsorships meaning some sort of secondary income is there for companies.
